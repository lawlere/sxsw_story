Things noticed alone

Tales from SXSW 2013 told through pictures.

Nobody notices what they donâ€™t notice. But I notice how much more I notice when alone.

It's less about who I want to be to that person I'm with. And more about the things around me.

I see the energy pulse through a crowd as a woman embarrases herself on stage.

The beauty in the cracked, soggy pavement.

I notice how uncomfortable people are at the thought of going without technology for a few hours.

The carefree feeling of being on a crowded street of bars becomes a part of me.

Watching someone else be alone makes me feel as if I have something in common with that other silent observer.

It's not the crazy hair of a rockstar, but instead the wonder of 'who is he?'.

Time can be spent watching the tiny strokes being etched onto a wall of Graffiti, and then the mural as a whole.

I can feel the knowing look of the poet, writing on the side of a road.

The mustard yellow of a plastic bush erupting from the hand of a beautiful woman.

The surreal feeling of living through a cloudy, dream-like existence never subsides. And I noticed it all.

The end.
